# Progressive profile visibility (Meetra)

**Progressive profile visibility** is a core product pattern: not “one profile everywhere,” but a **layered profile** that changes by phase. It fits **UX** (progressive disclosure — show the most important info first, reveal more as context and trust increase; less clutter and cognitive load) and **safety** (dating-platform research: privacy controls and limiting early personal disclosure reduce risk).

Aligned with event-networking products: pre-, during-, and post-event engagement differ; attendee data improves connection quality across the lifecycle.

**North-star rule:** A good Meetra profile answers **different questions at different moments:**

| Phase | Question the profile answers |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Before event** | Would I be comfortable meeting this person there? |
| **During event** | How do I start a fun conversation with this person? |
| **After event** | Do I want to keep seeing this person? |

---

## Three-layer profile system

### 1. Lite Profile — before the event

**Purpose:** Help people feel comfortable joining, get a quick vibe, reduce pre-event over-judgment. Small, visual, low-risk.

**Include:**

- First name only, or first name + initial
- Age range or exact age
- 1 main photo
- 3 short tags
- 1 fun prompt answer
- **Event intention** (e.g. “Looking to meet new people”, “Open to dating”, “Just seeing what happens”)
- Optional: zodiac / city district / language badges

**Do not show yet:**

- Full gallery, detailed bio
- Job / company, exact neighborhood
- Instagram / socials
- Deep preference filters

**Why:** Before the event, users should not feel like a swipe app. The profile should create **curiosity**, not full pre-screening.

---

### 2. Social Profile — during the event

**Purpose:** **Conversation fuel.** The profile acts like a conversation-starter card, not a résumé.

**Include:**

- Everything from Lite Profile
- 2–4 photos total
- 3 prompt answers
- “Talk to me about…” section
- Interests as playful chips
- Green flags / vibe descriptors
- **“Today at this event”** status (e.g. “Ask me about travel”, “Down for quiz games”, “Need help breaking the ice”)
- **Icebreaker card** generated by the app (“We both like…”, “Ask them about…”, “You two differ on…”)

**Optional but powerful:**

- Activity participation badges
- Compatibility hints unlocked after scan/game
- “Met already” marker after QR interaction

**Do not show yet:**

- Who liked whom, exact like counts
- Anything too evaluative or popularity-based on the profile itself

**Design question to optimize:** *What can I say to this person right now?*

**During-event UI:** Should feel more like a **social mission card** than a dating profile (e.g. “You both love live music”, “Try this opener: …”, “Unlocked after scan: …”). The profile is part of the gameplay.

**Unlock rule (recommended):** Social layer unlocks after at least one of: **QR scan**, same game/group assignment, mutual interaction milestone. This **rewards real interaction**.

---

### 3. Full Profile — after the event

**Purpose:** **Match decision + trust-building** after shared real-life context.

**Include:**

- Full photo set, full bio, deeper prompts
- Lifestyle basics, dating intentions, dealbreaker / preference basics
- **Interaction recap** (e.g. “You met during Trivia”, “You scanned each other at 8:42 PM”, “You completed 2 activities together”)
- Mutual interest signals only when relevant
- Verified badges / safety signals

**Optional:**

- Voice intro, short video intro
- Private profile notes (user-only)
- Post-event reflection cards (“felt easy to talk to”, “want to see again”, “friend vibe”, “romantic vibe”)

**Visibility:** Full profile to people you **interacted with**; like / like-back flow; chat on mutual like. Optional host/premium: only interacted users in post-event stack vs full attendee list.

---

## Six content blocks (how to structure data)

### A. Identity block

Lightweight and human: first name, age, pronouns (optional), photos, verification badge, orientation / who they want to meet (if relevant to event). **Top card.**

### B. Vibe block

More useful than a long bio: 3–5 vibe tags, one-line self-description, **energy mode** (calm / playful / outgoing / deep talker), **event mood** (mingling / flirting / making friends first). Helps self-sort socially without feeling clinical.

### C. Conversation block

**Heart of the product:** 2–3 prompt answers, “ask me about”, “unexpected fact”, “currently obsessed with”, “ideal first date”, “controversial opinion”, etc. Matters more than demographics because the product is built around **in-person interaction**.

### D. Compatibility block

Minimum needed: relationship intention; optional drinking/smoking, wants kids, pet person, travel, early bird/night owl, love language, social style. **Do not overload** — too much feels like a static swipe app again.

### E. Event block

What makes Meetra different: joined via event, activities completed, people met count, badges, compatibility clues unlocked, “you crossed paths in…” history. Profile is part of the **event experience**.

### F. Safety and trust block

Quiet in UI: photo/account verified, report/block, privacy controls, **what is visible at each phase**, option to hide profile after event ends. **Build in from the start**, not bolted on later.

---

## Recommended visibility model (summary)

| Phase | Visible to attendees | Hidden / deferred |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Before** | First name, age, main photo, 3 tags, 1 prompt, intention badge | Full bio, full gallery, lifestyle depth, likes |
| **During** (after unlock) | + more photos, prompts, shared-interest hints, app-generated openers | Likes, popularity metrics on profile |
| **After** (to people you interacted with) | Full profile, recap, like flow, chat on mutual like | (Per privacy settings) |

---

## Visual direction

**Not** Tinder or LinkedIn. Prefer: **card-based**, rounded, warm, clean, **playful data** not dense data.

**Layout sketch:**

- **Top:** photo, name, age, quick tags, verification
- **Middle:** big prompt cards, interest chips, event-specific card, “talk to me about”
- **Bottom:** compatibility basics, activity badges, trust/safety actions

---

## What to avoid

- Profile optimized for superficial **ranking before** the event
- Too long, too filter-heavy, too revealing too early
- Too close to a traditional dating app

If users can fully judge everyone before arriving, **you lose the magic of the event**.

---

## V1 profile spec (ship first)

**Lite:** first name, age, 1 photo, 3 tags, 1 prompt, intention badge.

**During (after unlock):** +2 photos, +2 prompts, interest chips, talk-to-me-about, one generated opener.

**Post-event full:** full gallery, bio, lifestyle basics, dating intention, event recap, mutual like/chat flow.

Enough to feel **differentiated** without over-complicating.
